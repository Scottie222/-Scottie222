### 1. I Can Design and Implement an ISMS From Scratch

**Project: [OpenVantage-ISMS](https://github.com/Scottie222/OpenVantage-ISMS)**

This is not a fictional scenario, this is my actual employer. Open Vantage (Pty) Ltd operates across South Africa, the Netherlands and the United Kingdom, handling enterprise client IP including source code repositories, AI systems and customer personal information under POPIA, GDPR and UK GDPR simultaneously. Two enterprise clients made ISO 27001:2022 certification a contractual requirement, triggering the project.

The tool I built generates a complete ISMS documentation suite: six core policies (Information Security Policy, Acceptable Use Policy, Access Control Policy, Incident Response Policy, Remote Working Policy and Data Classification Policy), a full Statement of Applicability covering all 93 ISO 27001:2022 Annex A controls with applicability justifications, current implementation status and evidence requirements, an ISMS Scope Document, and a Compliance Gap Report with a prioritised remediation roadmap.

This demonstrates that I understand ISO 27001:2022 deeply enough to apply it to a real multi-jurisdictional technology business — not just as a student exercise.

### 2. I Can Conduct a Formal ISO 27001 Risk Assessment

**Project: [StandardBank-Risk-Assessment](https://github.com/Scottie222/StandardBank-Risk-Assessment)**

This project assesses 16 risks across 12 information assets in the context of the real Experian SA breach of August 2020 — where social engineering exposed 24 million South African personal records and 793,749 businesses. Standard Bank was confirmed as one of the impacted institutions.

### 4. I Can Build and Test a Business Continuity Programme

**Project: [LifeHealthcare-BCP](https://github.com/Scottie222/LifeHealthcare-BCP)**

This project is built around the real June 2020 ransomware attack on Life Healthcare Group — South Africa's second-largest private hospital operator with 66 hospitals across 7 provinces. The attack encrypted admissions systems, billing systems and email servers. Life Healthcare activated manual fallback processes, maintained patient care throughout 28 days of downtime, and restored systems without paying the ransom.

The tool generates a full ISO 22301:2019-aligned Business Impact Analysis, a Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Plan with a 10-step DR runbook, and the feature I am most proud of — an **interactive tabletop exercise simulator**. You run it with `python run_bcp.py --sim` and you play the CISO of Life Healthcare as the ransomware unfolds in real time. Every decision you make is scored against what Life Healthcare actually did. A detailed exercise report is generated at the end.

This demonstrates that I understand BCP/DR not just as a document exercise but as a tested, living programme — which is exactly what ISO 22301 and most enterprise GRC frameworks require.

### 5. I Can Manage Third-Party Vendor Risk

**Project: [VendorRisk-SA](https://github.com/Scottie222/VendorRisk-SA)**

Third-party risk caused the Experian SA breach, the Dis-Chem breach, the TransUnion SA breach and the MTN breach — four of the most significant cybersecurity incidents in South African history. This project builds a vendor risk scoring tool that evaluates 10 vendors across 7 weighted security domains, tracks POPIA Section 21 operator agreement compliance, flags ISO 27001 certification gaps, monitors contract expiry dates and generates a full remediation roadmap.

Every vendor in the tool is mapped to a real SA breach incident. PayLink Processing SA — the most dangerous vendor in the assessment — processes card and banking data with no POPIA operator agreement, no ISO 27001 certification and the lowest possible scores across security controls, incident response and regulatory compliance. This is not a fictional worst-case scenario. This profile describes a real pattern seen in South African SME vendor ecosystems every day.

This demonstrates that I understand the POPIA operator relationship framework, ISO 27001 Annex A.5.19 supplier security requirements and the practical challenge of managing third-party risk across a diverse vendor portfolio.

### 6. I Can Design and Measure a Security Awareness Programme

**Project: [RetailCo-Security-Awareness](https://github.com/Scottie222/RetailCo-Security-Awareness)**

Human error causes 95% of South African data breaches. Phishing attacks cost SA organisations R200 million in 2023 — a 50% increase from the previous year. Despite this, most SA organisations still run annual security awareness training and consider the box ticked. This project demonstrates a better approach.

The tool tracks three phishing simulation campaigns run over Q1–Q3 2024 across 8 departments and 450 employees, using templates based on real SA attack vectors: SARS tax refund impersonation, FNB banking security alerts and CEO fraud / Business Email Compromise. It tracks click rates, report rates and training completion per department per quarter, measures improvement over time, identifies the highest-risk departments and generates a full ISO 27001 A.6.3 aligned awareness report.

The headline result: average click rate dropped from 35.8% to 16.6% over three quarters — a 19.2% reduction. This demonstrates that a well-designed, continuously measured awareness programme produces real, quantifiable behaviour change.

### 7. I Can Assess Cloud Security Posture

**Project: [CloudSec-Assessment-SA](https://github.com/Scottie222/CloudSec-Assessment-SA)**

My CV shows AWS and GCP experience from Barbed Era and my home lab. This project proves it. The tool evaluates 25 security checks across AWS and Azure environments against CIS Benchmark controls (CIS AWS Foundations v2.0 and CIS Azure Foundations v2.0) and ISO 27001:2022 Annex A.5.23.

It identifies 7 critical findings in the simulated FinTechSA environment including root account MFA disabled, wildcard IAM policies giving complete account access, a publicly accessible S3 bucket containing customer KYC documents, Azure Blob Storage with anonymous access enabled, and security groups allowing unrestricted SSH and RDP from the entire internet. Each finding includes the specific CIS control reference, the ISO 27001 control, the POPIA compliance impact, and a clear remediation step.

This demonstrates that I understand cloud security not just conceptually but at the configuration level — the difference between what the CIS Benchmark requires and what is actually deployed, and why that gap matters under POPIA Section 19.
